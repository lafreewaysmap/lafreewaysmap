A Circumnavigation of Majesty: Exploring the Circle Tour of Lake Superior

Lake Superior, the largest freshwater lake in the world by surface area, is a majestic body of water that holds within its depths a wealth of natural beauty and historical significance. Its shores, sculpted by glaciers and time, offer a tapestry of diverse landscapes, from rugged cliffs and dense forests to tranquil beaches and charming towns. A journey around Lake Superior, often referred to as the "Circle Tour," presents a unique opportunity to immerse oneself in this captivating region, unraveling its secrets and experiencing its grandeur firsthand.

The Circle Tour: A Journey of Discovery

The Circle Tour encompasses the entire perimeter of Lake Superior, traversing over 1,300 miles (2,100 kilometers) across four states: Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, and Ontario, Canada. This scenic route weaves through a variety of landscapes, each offering distinct experiences and attractions.

The North Shore of Minnesota:

The North Shore of Minnesota, renowned for its dramatic shoreline, is a highlight of the Circle Tour. Towering cliffs, sculpted by ancient glaciers, plunge into the depths of Lake Superior, creating breathtaking vistas. The Superior Hiking Trail, a 310-mile (499-kilometer) footpath, traverses this dramatic coastline, offering stunning views and opportunities for hiking and camping. The region is also home to numerous state parks, including Gooseberry Falls State Park, Tettegouche State Park, and Split Rock Lighthouse State Park, each showcasing the unique beauty of the North Shore.

The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore:

Further west, the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, an archipelago of 21 islands in Lake Superior, offers a serene escape. These islands, once home to indigenous cultures and later, logging camps, now provide refuge for diverse wildlife and offer opportunities for kayaking, sailing, and exploring the historic lighthouses that dot their shores.

The Wisconsin Northwoods:

Moving eastward, the Circle Tour enters the Wisconsin Northwoods, a vast expanse of forested wilderness. This region, dotted with pristine lakes and rivers, is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for fishing, boating, and exploring the numerous state parks and forests, including Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest and Apostle Islands National Lakeshore.

The Upper Peninsula of Michigan:

The Upper Peninsula of Michigan, known for its rugged beauty and rich history, offers a unique blend of natural wonders and cultural attractions. The scenic Tahquamenon Falls State Park, featuring two magnificent waterfalls, is a must-see. The region also boasts the Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore, where colorful sandstone cliffs rise dramatically from the lake, offering breathtaking views and opportunities for kayaking and hiking.

The Canadian Side: Ontario, Canada

The Circle Tour continues across the border into Ontario, Canada, where the landscape transforms into a mix of rugged cliffs, lush forests, and charming towns. The Pukaskwa National Park, known for its pristine wilderness and ancient boreal forest, offers opportunities for hiking, camping, and exploring the rugged shoreline. The region also boasts the historic town of Sault Ste. Marie, a gateway to the eastern end of Lake Superior.

The Circle Tour of Lake Superior offers a unique opportunity to experience the diverse beauty of this vast region, from the rugged cliffs of the North Shore to the serene islands of the Apostle Islands and the historic charm of the Upper Peninsula. Here are some of the key benefits of embarking on this journey:

  • Immersion in Nature: The Circle Tour provides an immersive experience in nature, allowing travelers to witness the raw beauty of Lake Superior and its surrounding landscapes. From the towering cliffs of the North Shore to the tranquil waters of the Apostle Islands, the journey offers a constant stream of awe-inspiring vistas.

  • Exploration of Diverse Landscapes: The Circle Tour traverses a variety of landscapes, each offering unique experiences and attractions. Travelers can explore rugged shorelines, dense forests, pristine lakes, and historic towns, each contributing to a rich and varied journey.

  • Outdoor Recreation Opportunities: The Circle Tour is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for hiking, kayaking, fishing, camping, and exploring the numerous state parks and national forests along the route.

  • Cultural Immersion: The Circle Tour offers glimpses into the rich history and culture of the region, from the indigenous heritage of the North Shore to the logging camps of the Apostle Islands and the mining history of the Upper Peninsula.

  • Unique and Memorable Experiences: The Circle Tour offers a unique and unforgettable experience, allowing travelers to create lasting memories and appreciate the beauty and grandeur of Lake Superior.

FAQs about the Circle Tour of Lake Superior:

Q: How long does it take to complete the Circle Tour?

A: The duration of the Circle Tour depends on the pace of travel and the chosen route. A typical drive around the lake can take anywhere from 10 to 14 days, allowing for stops at various points of interest along the way.

Q: What is the best time of year to take the Circle Tour?

A: The best time to take the Circle Tour depends on personal preferences and the desired activities. Spring and fall offer vibrant foliage and mild weather, while summer provides the best opportunities for water activities. Winter offers a unique experience, with snow-covered landscapes and opportunities for snowshoeing and ice fishing.

Q: What are some of the must-see attractions along the Circle Tour?

A: Some of the must-see attractions along the Circle Tour include:

  • North Shore of Minnesota: Superior Hiking Trail, Gooseberry Falls State Park, Tettegouche State Park, Split Rock Lighthouse State Park.
  • Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Apostle Islands, Madeline Island, lighthouses.
  • Wisconsin Northwoods: Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest, Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, numerous lakes and rivers.
  • Upper Peninsula of Michigan: Tahquamenon Falls State Park, Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore, Mackinac Island, Seney National Wildlife Refuge.
  • Ontario, Canada: Pukaskwa National Park, Sault Ste. Marie, Agawa Canyon Tour Train.

  • Pack Appropriately: Pack for a variety of weather conditions and outdoor activities, including hiking boots, warm clothing, and rain gear.
